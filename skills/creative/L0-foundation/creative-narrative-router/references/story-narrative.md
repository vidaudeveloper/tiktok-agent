# story_narrative — Story-led commercial

> ID: `story_narrative`  
> Use when: plot/emotion drives the piece; product may appear organically, not as hero.

## When to use

- “Story”, “mini film”, “emotional”, “soft sell”, “not a product demo”
- Lead character: relationship, choice, feeling
- 16–90s

## When not to use

- Feature list in 30s → `product_ad`
- Clear pain → product without story ask → `problem_solution`

## Structure — compressed three acts

| Act | Beat | Role |
|-----|------|------|
| I | `setup` | Character + world + tension (~5–25% runtime) |
| II | `turn` | Event/conflict (~25–75%) |
| III | `payoff` | Emotional landing; optional `brand_touch` |

No hard sell VO on last frame unless user requests conversion tone.

## Beat map — 30s

| Beat | Duration | Visual |
|------|----------|--------|
| `setup` | 0–8s | Establish character & normal world |
| `inciting_moment` | 8–14s | Small disruption |
| `development` | 14–22s | Reaction + action |
| `payoff` | 22–28s | Resolution / feeling |
| `brand_touch` | 28–30s | Optional product/logo — light |

## Scene types

`character_moment` · `environment_story` · `prop_reveal` · `closing_image`

## Narrative Driver

Who + situation + conflict (therefore/but, not “and then…”) + emotional payoff + how product/brand appears (if any).

## Forbidden

- “This is a story about…” opener
- Flat event lists
- Turning story into SKU spec sheet without permission
- Invented products

## Voiceover (~150 wpm)

30s: ~40–55 words (sparse). 60s: ~80–110 words.

## Example — commuter day, earbuds as prop

| # | Beat | Shot |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | setup | Rush-hour stress |
| 2 | inciting_moment | Noise hits |
| 3 | development | Puts earbuds on, calmer |
| 4 | payoff | Lighter walk out |
| 5 | brand_touch | 1s product beauty shot |
